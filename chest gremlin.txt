theme: diluted fantasy, loosely wow, osrs but much more messy, look below for history

genre: idle, clicker, roguelike

the goal of the game is to make it to the grafter and increase your prestige

> start as a grem or gnome

you can recruit a pack animal > brahmin, camel

in chest grem you find chests and later open them

you can identify an item which reveals type (corrupted, holy, sharp, etc)

you are either in a [hostile area] (dungeons, forts, etc)
or you're in        [safety] (pumpkin farm, old tower, etc)

hostile area always goes to safety and safety to a hostile area <->

you can cower from a run but miss out on better chests

when in [safety] you can [set up shop] which attracts visitors

visitors will bid on items and you can accept or want more for it

||camp reputation|| affects how gullible visitors are

shoppers have a race and traits:
a hobbit can arrive but he's corrupted, wounded, rich-looking, etc, and you can negotiate price based on this

the history i promised:
you are in stone lands, a somewhat chaotic realm struck by disorder. heroes roam freely in search of xp and gear. you are a gremlin or gnome and know a bit how to fight, find chests, circumvent danger, and to set up shop. the world feels like a free for all, with rampant heroism, strive and riches all taking place. your goal is to reach the grafter and show him that you've honored the tinker, so you can prestige and do it again.

here is a train of thought that makes chest gremlin what it is:
to work it out:
gnome walks thru the dungeon, even tho heroes are meant to for xp and loot
if he's an npc vendor, what's he doing? he can loot gear left behind
so to step in the footsteps of heroes, and hide in holes and corners
until the heroes leave
this way, he's still the vendor, still the sneaky little gremlin
he could watch from behind a crate or a sewer outlet
when he comes out he grabs stuff left behind
kind of an experimental game that takes some getting used to
requires some wrapping your head around
the idea behind chest gremlin was never "npc vendor"
you still need gear to fight off creatures like yourself, like big rats
who are sniffing when the hero has cleared a dungeon and left items behind
this can be easily auto-gamed --->

and with that said, chest grem can be auto-gamed
first, the player quote unquote checks every room and hallway
and cowers to the nearest hidey hole possible, hopes to not get discovered by heroes
the grem inspects items and stashes it when they're trinkets
and drags them into sewer holes when they're bigger, waiting for them to wash out
at the outlet in a worse condition in a slickened quality
he can also drag items back to the entrance of the dungeon
to his pet pack animal camel and then return back into the dungeon
but this is a test of INT and a risk trade off, since backtracking
introduces risks of meeting other heroes, creatures, and traps
you can tag on more systems unto this
path of ahcra is an example of a roguelike that mixes auto genres --->
